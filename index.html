💳 Nobody Taught Us This

MONEY
101

Overdrafts, credit cards, Plum, Zilch — what actually are these things? Explained simply, no jargon, no judgement. Everyone starts somewhere.

📖 What Is An Overdraft?

OVERDRAFTS EXPLAINED

An overdraft lets you spend more money than you actually have in your bank account — your balance goes below £0. Think of it as a short-term buffer from your bank.

For people on benefits, a basic bank account overdraft can cover you in a genuine emergency — but be careful. Unlike student overdrafts, most standard accounts charge interest on overdrafts (often 39–40% EAR). If you're overdrawn and struggling, call your bank and ask about hardship support or a fee-free buffer — many offer one if you ask directly.

Always treat an overdraft as a last resort emergency fund — not a way to stretch your spending. Getting stuck in a permanent overdraft with interest charges is one of the hardest financial holes to climb out of.

📖 What Is A Credit Card?

CREDIT CARDS EXPLAINED

A credit card lets you buy things now and pay for them later — usually at the end of the month. The card company pays the shop, then you pay the card company back.

Used right, they help: you get consumer protections, can handle genuine emergencies, and can slowly build your credit score (which matters for renting).

Used wrong, they spiral: interest rates of 20–40% mean a small debt can double. The absolute golden rule — only ever spend on a credit card what you already have in your bank account, and pay the full balance every month. If you can only make minimum payments, a credit card is not the right tool right now.

💡 Credit Score — Why It Matters

BUILD YOUR CREDIT SCORE

Your credit score affects your ability to rent a home, get a phone contract, and access better financial products. Even on benefits, you can improve it for free right now.

Register on the electoral roll at your current address — this alone gives a meaningful boost and takes 5 minutes at gov.uk/register-to-vote. Pay any bills you have on time. Check your score free at Experian, ClearScore, or Credit Karma — and check for errors, as they're more common than people think and can be corrected for free.

Avoid Payday Loans

Payday loans charge 1,000–1,500% APR — they are a debt trap designed to keep people borrowing. If you're in a cash crisis, try your bank hardship team, Citizens Advice, or a credit union first. Always.

⚡ Secret Energy Tricks

GAS &
ELECTRIC

Most people on benefits don't know these tricks exist. They do. Use them — they are free money and free help that's yours by right.

🎟️

CAB Fuel Vouchers

Citizens Advice Bureau can provide fuel vouchers to help with gas and electric bills — and you can ask for them up to 3 times within any 6-month period. Almost nobody knows this. Go to your local CAB or call them and ask specifically about fuel vouchers. Don't be embarrassed — it's what they're there for.

💡 3 times in 6 months — completely free
🔋

Emergency Credit From Your Supplier

If you're on a prepayment meter and can't top up — call your energy supplier and ask for emergency credit. Every major supplier in the UK offers it. They add credit to your meter immediately. You pay it back gradually when you next top up. Never go cold, never go without cooking facilities. Just call and ask — this is one of the best-kept secrets going.

📞 Call your supplier — ask right now
🌡️

Warm Home Discount — £150

If you're on a qualifying benefit like Pension Credit, Universal Credit, or Income Support, you may be entitled to £150 off your energy bill through the Warm Home Discount scheme. Check eligibility via your energy supplier's website — some get it automatically, others need to apply before the deadline each year.

💷 £150 off your bill — check now
🏠

ECO4 — Free Insulation & Boilers

The ECO4 scheme provides free home insulation, boiler upgrades, and energy improvements for people on qualifying benefits. It's a government scheme and can save hundreds of pounds a year on bills. Check your eligibility at gov.uk or through your energy supplier.

🆓 Free home upgrades — government scheme
💰

Social Energy Tariffs

Some energy suppliers offer special low-cost "social tariffs" for people on low incomes or benefits — but they don't always advertise them. Call your supplier and ask directly: "Do you have a social tariff or hardship rate for customers on benefits?" The answer might surprise you.

☎️ Ask your supplier directly
🤝

Call Before You Struggle

If you're falling behind on energy bills, call your supplier before it becomes a crisis. All major suppliers are legally required to offer payment plans and debt support. Calling early means more options. Waiting until you're cut off makes everything harder.

⚖️ They must help by law

🏠 Keeping A Roof Over Your Head

HOUSING
RIGHTS

Housing rights for people on benefits are stronger than most realise. Know your rights and use the free services available.

🏛️

Shelter — Free Legal Help

Shelter provides free housing advice and legal support. If you're facing eviction, repossession, or homelessness — contact Shelter before anything else. They specialise in benefit claimant situations.

📞 Helpline: 0808 800 4444 (free)
💷

Housing Benefit / LHA

If you're renting privately, check your Local Housing Allowance rate — the government contribution toward rent. Many people don't claim the full amount they're entitled to. Check via your council website.

🔎 Check your LHA rate today
🚫

Know Your Tenancy Rights

Even on benefits, you have the same rights as any tenant. Landlords cannot evict you without proper notice. Don't leave voluntarily if threatened — always get Shelter advice first.

⚠️ Don't leave without advice first
🏠

Council Housing Register

If you're in insecure housing, register on your local council's housing list as soon as possible. Waiting times are long but the clock only starts when you register. Do it today — not next week.

🗓️ Register now — waits are long
🧘 Feeling Overwhelmed?

ANXIETY &
STRESS

Living on benefits is genuinely stressful — the system is hard, money is tight, and it takes a real toll. You're not broken. You're human. These tools actually help.

🕯️ SELF-PROTECTION RITUAL — 2 MINUTE RESET

When the stress and anxiety hits and your mind is racing, try this simple grounding practice.

1. Find somewhere quiet. Close your eyes.
2. Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat.
3. Take 3 slow deep breaths — in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6.
4. Repeat quietly to yourself, as many times as you need:
"I am safe. I am protected. I am loved."

This grounding technique brings you back to the present moment and calms your nervous system. It works.

🌬️

Box Breathing

In for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times. Used by the military under extreme pressure — works just as well for benefits stress and anxiety spirals.

⚡ Works in under 2 minutes
👋

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. Instantly pulls you out of panic and back into the present moment.

🧠 Classic grounding technique
🚶

Move Your Body

A 10-minute walk outside genuinely reduces cortisol levels. The park is free, fresh air is free, and both genuinely help lift your mood immediately.

🌿 Free and instant
😴

Protect Your Sleep

Everything feels worse when you're exhausted. Anxiety, low mood, and hopelessness are all amplified by bad sleep. Even small improvements to your routine make a big difference.

💤 7–9 hours is the target
✍️

Journal It Out

Write down what's worrying you. Getting thoughts out of your head and onto paper reduces their power significantly. Even 5 minutes of free writing helps enormously.

📓 No rules — just write
🗣️

Talk to Someone

Keeping it all inside makes everything worse. Tell a friend, family member, GP, or counsellor how you're feeling. You deserve support — free options are listed below.
