🍎 The Biggest Struggle

FOOD
COSTS

Food prices have shot up and benefits haven't kept up. Here's how people across the UK are actually eating well on almost nothing.

01
Plan your meals weeklyWrite a meal plan before you shop. Buy only what you need — no waste, no last-minute expensive shops.
02
Shop at Aldi firstAldi is consistently cheapest in the UK. Do your main shop there, small top-ups at Tesco only when needed.
03
Never shop hungrySounds obvious — it works. A full stomach means you stick to the list and spend far less. Every penny counts.
04
Cook from scratchBatch cooking saves both time and money. Rice, pasta, lentils, eggs, tinned tomatoes — cheap, filling, and nutritious.
05
Best days: Sun & MonQuieter stores and more yellow-sticker reduced items. Sunday evenings especially great for bargains.
06
Make your own lunchA packed lunch saves £5–£8 a day compared to buying out. Over a month that's over £100 back in your pocket.

💷 Making It Stretch

MONEY &
BENEFITS

Millions of people are missing out on benefits they're entitled to. Make sure you're getting everything you're owed before cutting back on anything else.

🔍

Check What You're Owed

An estimated £19 billion in benefits goes unclaimed every year in the UK. Use the Entitledto or Turn2Us calculators to check exactly what you should be getting. It takes 10 minutes.

💡 £19 billion unclaimed every year
🏛️

Citizens Advice — Free Help

Citizens Advice offers completely free, independent help with benefits, debt, housing, and employment. Walk in or use their website. They know the system inside out — use them.

✅ 100% free, totally confidential
💳

Universal Credit Tips

If you're on UC, request an advance payment if you're waiting for your first payment. Also check if you qualify for Council Tax Reduction — many people don't know it exists.

⚡ Ask about hardship payments too
📋

Budgeting Basics

Write down every penny coming in and going out. Even a rough budget on paper reveals where money disappears. Knowing is the first step — don't be scared to look.

📝 Awareness = control
💡

Cut Energy Bills

Apply for the Warm Home Discount (£150 off energy) if you're on qualifying benefits. Also check if you're eligible for free insulation and boiler upgrades through the ECO4 scheme.

🔥 Warm Home Discount = £150 off
📞

Call Before You Cut

Before cancelling anything — phone the company first. Energy firms, broadband providers, and councils often have hardship rates, payment holidays, or social tariffs they don't advertise.

☎️ Always ask — the worst is no
🏠 Keeping A Roof Over Your Head

HOUSING
RIGHTS

Housing rights for people on benefits are stronger than most realise. Know your rights and use the free services available to you.

🏛️

Shelter — Free Legal Help

Shelter provides free housing advice and legal support. If you're facing eviction, repossession, or homelessness — contact Shelter before anything else. They have specialist advisers for benefit claimants.

📞 Helpline: 0808 800 4444
💷

Housing Benefit / LHA

If you're renting privately, check your Local Housing Allowance rate — the government contribution toward rent. Many people don't claim the full amount they're entitled to. Check via your council website.

🔎 Check your LHA rate
🚫

Read Your Tenancy

Even on benefits, you have the same rights as any tenant. Landlords cannot evict you without proper notice. Don't leave voluntarily if threatened — always get advice from Shelter first.

⚠️ Know your rights before you move
🏠

Council Housing Register

If you're in insecure housing, register on your local council's housing list as soon as possible. Waiting times are long but the clock only starts when you register. Do it today.

🗓️ Register now — waits are long
🧘 Feeling Overwhelmed?

ANXIETY &
STRESS

Living on benefits is genuinely stressful — the system is hard, money is tight, and it takes a toll. You're not broken. You're human. These tools actually help.

🕯️ SELF-PROTECTION RITUAL — 2 MINUTE RESET

When the stress and anxiety hits and your mind is racing, try this simple grounding practice.

1. Find somewhere quiet. Close your eyes.
2. Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat.
3. Take 3 slow deep breaths — in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6.
4. Repeat quietly to yourself, as many times as you need:
"I am safe. I am protected. I am loved."

This grounding technique brings you back to the present moment and calms your nervous system. It works.

🌬️

Box Breathing

In for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times. Used by the military under extreme pressure — works just as well for benefits stress and anxiety spirals.

⚡ Works in under 2 minutes
👋

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. Instantly pulls you out of panic and back into the present moment.

🧠 Classic grounding technique
🚶

Move Your Body

A 10-minute walk outside genuinely reduces cortisol levels. You don't need a gym. The park is free, fresh air is free, and both genuinely help lift your mood.

🌿 Free and instant
😴

Protect Your Sleep

Everything feels worse when you're exhausted. Anxiety, low mood, and hopelessness are all amplified by bad sleep. Even small improvements to sleep routine make a big difference.

💤 7–9 hours is the target
✍️

Journal It Out

Write down what's worrying you. Getting thoughts out of your head and onto paper reduces their power significantly. Even 5 minutes of free writing helps enormously.

📓 No rules — just write
🗣️

Talk to Someone

Keeping it all inside makes everything worse. Tell a friend, family member, GP, or counsellor how you're feeling. You deserve support — free options are listed below.

💬 You don't have to do this alone
💚 Your Wellbeing Matters

STAYING
WELL

When money is tight it's easy to let your own wellbeing slip. Here are completely free things that genuinely make a difference.

📚

Libraries Are Free

Books, internet, warmth, and community — all free. Most libraries also run free wellbeing groups and activities.

🌳

Get Outside

Parks, canals, walking routes — nature is free and scientifically proven to reduce anxiety and improve mood.

🤝

Volunteer Locally

Gives you routine, purpose, and community. Many volunteering roles lead to paid work. A genuine game-changer.

🧘

Free Yoga & Meditation

YouTube has thousands of free guided sessions. Even 10 minutes of mindfulness daily changes how you handle stress.

☎️

Stay Connected

Loneliness and poverty often come together. Reach out to one person this week — a text, a call, anything. Connection matters.

🎨

Create Something

Drawing, writing, cooking, gardening — creative activities rebuild confidence and give your brain a healthy focus.

💊

Free Prescriptions

If you're on Universal Credit, Income Support, or other qualifying benefits — you get free NHS prescriptions. Claim what you're owed.

🦷

Free NHS Dental

People on qualifying benefits are entitled to free NHS dental treatment. Many don't know this. Check your entitlement now.

🚨 Panic Attack? Do This Now

If you feel one coming — stop. Sit or lie down. Do 3 rounds of box breathing (in 4, hold 4, out 4, hold 4). Remind yourself: this feeling will pass. It always does. You are safe. You have survived every difficult day so far.

This feeling will pass You are safe right now Breathe — just breathe
